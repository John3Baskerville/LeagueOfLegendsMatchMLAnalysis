Artificial Neural Network Project¶

Objective¶

League of Legends is a competitive online battle arena where two teams of 5 players and their unique characters face off on a map. The objective of each match is to infiltrate the enemy team base and destroy its nexus. Each match lasts around 30 minutes and the depth of gameplay requires cooperation, management of resources, and strategy in order to win a match. The objective of this project is to use a ranked game dataset and its features to categorize the results of a match as either a win or a loss for either the red or blue team.

Source URL¶

Dataset URL: https://www.kaggle.com/bobbyscience/league-of-legends-diamond-ranked-games-10-min

Input variables and Preprocessing steps¶

There 2 teams with 19 features each. Each row represents 1 game with collected data from the first 10 minutes. I expected some of the features to be highly correlated. Events such as takedowns of other players and completing in-game objectives all grant gold and gold is one of the most important resources to help characters progress in each match.

After looking at a heatmap that showed correlations between features it was easy to remove features that were complicating the model. To shrink the number of features the final model used to train, I removed one of each couple features that had a correlation score higher than 0.8 or smaller than -0.8.

The dataset has 9,879 rows all used to train the models.

The column ‘blueWins’ is the dependent variable and a value of 1 represents a win for the blue team.

Final Model and Training Algorithm summary¶

The final model was aquired after using GridSearchCV to optimize paremters and find and estimate the appropriate layers and nodes.

The final model shape had 3 Dense layers. 1 input layer with the specified input dimension matching the number of features the model had and two additional dense layers where the nodes were eventually randomly picked. 1 dropout layer was also included inbetween the 2nd and 3rd layer.

The model converged on an accuracy pretty quickly and most training was able to be completed within 20 epochs with a batch size of 100.

Metrics and justification¶

The metrics used in these models is binary_accuracy. The dependant variable in the data is represented by either a 0 or a 1 and the binary accuracy metric calculates how often predictions match binary labels.

Validation methods¶

The intial models were validated with a reserved dataset that was split at the initial preprocess step. This was a quick way to get the first few models tested and benchmarked.The sweetspot for validation allocation rested between 15% and 25% of the dataset.

When the final models were being tested, I decided to allow the GridSearch to use crossfold validation or kfold. The comprimise between time and accuracy came down to 3,4 and 5 folds for validating the data.

Optimization¶

I had to be careful not to add too many combinations of parameters to test. After narrowing in on the best activation functions to use, I was greedy with my approach and was forced to terminate a training session that was going over 7 hours. Computing limitations forced me to create optimization code that ran in the range of 0:00 - 25:00 minutes.

GridSearchCV is used to tune parameters. First, the activation function for the input and output layers were tested with 10 different combinations with the relevant activations below. This had a total run time of 0:22:35.514558 (H:mm:ss).

activations1 = ['softmax', 'softplus', 'softsign', 'sigmoid', 'hard_sigmoid']
activations2 = ['softmax','sigmoid']

Once the optimal activations were found the next step was to find an optimal Neural Network shape. The first test included experimenting with 3 models of various shapes. I tested models with 2 and 3 layer deep networks with nodes varying in the range of 10-25. My initial strategy was to focus on a network with nodes in a similar range to the feature length (21).

The initial winner of the shape training was a model with 2 layers with 20 and 10 nodes respectivly. This winning shape was used to test model paremters even further.

Next I tried to test too many parameters and epochs. 13. Final Discussion¶

The final results with 72% accuracy gives insight to how the matches in Leage of Legends play out. Matches range from 15-45 minutes so to be able to accuratley determine a matches outcome with data from the first 10 minutes suggests a games outcome can be determined much sooner than players realize and there might be some early game strategies that will help with chances of winning.

There is room for improvement with this model. After spending hours trying to determing the optimal combinations of layers and nodes the final model was decided with random testing and maunal tweeking. A more systytematic approach where much much more time is spent on optimizing paremters can push the model into a higher accuracy.
